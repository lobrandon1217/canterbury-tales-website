CANTERBURY

THE HOME OF THE CANTERBURY TALES

Why Canterbury, England?

Canterbury is the home of the Canterbury Cathedral, one of the oldest and most famous Christian structures in England. It is also the location of the death of Saint Thomas Becket, the Archbishop of Canterbury who was murdered in 1170, giving the location some history to look for.

You could also visit one of these other locations as a part of your vacation, but that's not all of them. For more information, ask a tour guide.

  • St. Augustine's Abbey - ruins of a 6th-century church
  • Canterbury Roman Museum - museum of Roman history, mosaics, and artifacts
  • Beaney's House of Art and Knowledge - museum, art gallery, and library
  • Westgate - historic gatehouse with a pub and nice views
  • Canterbury Castle - William the Conqueror's ruined 1070 castle
